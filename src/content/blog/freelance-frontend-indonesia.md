## Why Freelance Frontend?

Indonesia's digital economy is booming. Companies need websites, web apps, and landing pages — and many prefer freelancers over agencies for flexibility and cost.

**Demand areas in 2026:**
- Landing pages for startups (UMKM going digital)
- Company profile websites
- E-commerce frontends
- AI-integrated dashboards and admin panels
- Redesign existing sites with modern frameworks

For maintenance of existing sites, learn from [Migrating Legacy Frontends with AI](/blog/hermes-frontend-legacy-migration/).

## Step 1: Build a Strong Portfolio

Your portfolio is your most powerful sales tool. It should:

- **Show your best 3-5 projects** — quality over quantity
- **Explain your role** — what did you build, which stack, what challenges
- **Include live demos** — not just screenshots
- **Be fast** — 95+ Lighthouse score, SEO optimized

See [Astro Portfolio Tutorial: Build a Developer Portfolio](/blog/astro-portfolio-tutorial/) for step-by-step instructions.

### Portfolio Projects That Attract Clients

| Type | Examples | Why Clients Want It |
|------|----------|-------------------|
| Landing page | Saas landing, product page | High conversion focus |
| Company profile | Multi-page with CMS | Most common request |
| Dashboard | Analytics, admin panel | Complex UI skills |
| E-commerce | Product catalog + cart | Big budget projects |
| AI integration | Chat interface, AI search | Premium pricing |

4. **LinkedIn** — optimize profile for "Frontend Developer Indonesia", post regularly

6. **Direct outreach** — find companies with outdated sites, offer redesign

## Step 3: Set Your Rates

Typical freelance rates for Indonesian frontend developers in 2026:

| Level | Hourly (USD) | Project (Landing Page) |
|-------|-------------|----------------------|
| Junior (1-2yr) | $15-25 | $200-500 |
| Mid (3-5yr) | $25-45 | $500-1500 |
| Senior (5yr+) | $45-80 | $1500-5000 |
| AI Specialist | $60-100 | $3000+ |

**Tip:** Charge by project, not by hour. Projects reflect value delivered, not time spent. Use AI tools to deliver faster without lowering prices — that's your margin.

## Step 4: Use AI to Work Faster

AI tools let you deliver more value in less time:

- **Code generation** — [Claude](/blog/claude-api-frontend/) or [GPT-4o](/blog/vibecoding-tools/) for components and pages
- **Code review** — [Hermes Agent](/blog/ai-automated-code-review-hermes/) for automated PR checks
- **Design to code** — screenshot-to-code tools for rapid prototyping
- **Content writing** — AI for blog posts, case studies, proposals
- **Testing** — [AI-powered testing](/blog/ai-powered-accessibility-testing/) for faster QA

Master [vibecoding](/blog/vibecoding-intro/) and you'll deliver projects 2-3x faster than developers who type every line.

## Step 5: Scale Your Freelance Business

1. **Build a referral system** — give existing clients a reason to refer you
2. **Create a niche** — be the "React + AI" guy, not the general web developer
3. **Passive income** — sell templates, themes, or mini-courses
4. **Subcontract** — when you're fully booked, hire junior devs
5. **Retainer clients** — monthly maintenance + updates for recurring income

## Tech Stack for Indonesian Freelancers

**Must-have:**
- React (Next.js) or Vue (Nuxt) — pick one and master it
- Tailwind CSS — fastest way to build UIs, clients love the results
- WordPress — many existing clients need WP maintenance
- Responsive design — Indonesian mobile usage is 80%+

**Nice-to-have:**
- Laravel API — many Indonesian backends use Laravel
- Astro — perfect for portfolio/company sites
- AI integration — API calls to OpenAI, Claude, etc.

See [Full-Stack Next.js + Laravel](/blog/fullstack-nextjs-laravel/) for the ultimate freelance combo.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Underpricing** — your rate reflects your value, not your hours
- **No contract** — always use a written agreement
- **Scope creep** — define deliverables clearly before starting
- **No portfolio** — clients need proof, not promises
- **Ignoring mobile** — test every project on real mobile devices
